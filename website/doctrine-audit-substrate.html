Doctrine

Audit Substrate

A pre-submission audit substrate is the operational infrastructure that identifies documentation deficiencies before claims are submitted — not after they are denied. GAKK designs audit substrates as executable, staff-operable systems, not consultant-dependent review processes.

The Problem: Auditing After the Fact

Most FQHC audit activity is retrospective. Denials arrive, a sample of encounters is reviewed, deficiencies are identified, training is delivered, and the cycle repeats. This model generates accurate information about failures that have already happened. It does not prevent the next cycle of the same failures. Pre-submission auditing — review of documentation before claims are submitted — breaks the retrospective cycle by moving the correction point upstream of the denial.

What a Substrate Is

An audit substrate is not a periodic review conducted by an auditor. It is an operational system: defined scope, defined review criteria, defined decision logic, and a defined feedback path. It specifies what gets reviewed, when, by whom, against what criteria, and what happens when a deficiency is found. It is executable by trained staff without requiring external audit expertise for routine operation, and it handles the encounter volume that a periodic audit cannot.

Alignment to FQHC Requirements

The audit logic in an FQHC pre-submission substrate must be aligned to the specific requirements that govern FQHC billing: encounter rate documentation sufficiency, medical necessity for managed care payers, payer-specific documentation standards, and service type-specific requirements for behavioral health, dental, and enabling services. Audit logic built on generic evaluation and management criteria will miss the failures that matter most in an FQHC environment.

What GAKK Builds

We design pre-submission audit substrates aligned to the organization's payer mix, service types, and workflow capacity. This includes: encounter-level audit criteria derived from actual payer requirements and denial history; a review workflow that integrates with the organization's EHR and billing processes; a feedback protocol that delivers corrective action at the provider and encounter level before submission; and a governance schedule for maintaining and updating the substrate as payer requirements change. The substrate is designed to operate without GAKK after implementation.

If your organization is finding documentation deficiencies at the denial stage rather than before submission, a pre-submission audit substrate can break that cycle.
