Billing & Revenue Cycle

Revenue cycle failure in an FQHC is rarely a billing department problem in isolation. It is an operational alignment problem — documentation, intake, eligibility, and billing workflows that were never designed to work together under FQHC-specific conditions. GAKK addresses the full cycle.

Claim Submission

FQHC claim submission operates under rules that do not apply in standard fee-for-service environments: encounter rate billing, prospective payment settlement, managed care encounter submission requirements, and payer-specific variations that differ by contract. Claims that are technically correct by general billing standards can still be denied or underpaid in an FQHC if they are not aligned to the specific requirements of the applicable payer and contract.

GAKK reviews claim submission workflows against the organization's actual payer mix and contract terms, identifies submission logic gaps, and develops submission standards that account for FQHC-specific requirements by payer type. This is not a billing department replacement — it is the governance layer that ensures the billing department is working from accurate, current, and documented submission standards.

Denial Management

Denial management in an FQHC is a diagnostic function before it is a resolution function. The first step is not appeals — it is classification. Denials recorded only as denied produce no useful information. Denials classified by root cause, payer, provider, service type, and denial reason code produce the data needed to determine whether the failure originated in documentation, intake, eligibility, coding, or submission.

GAKK builds denial management systems that classify denials at the root cause level, route correction to the appropriate part of the organization, and track denial patterns over time to identify systemic failures. Appeals management is one component of this system — it is not the whole system.

Payment Posting & Accounts Receivable

Payment posting errors and accounts receivable mismanagement compound quietly. Incorrect payment posting produces misleading performance data, distorts accounts receivable aging, and allows underpayment to go undetected. Accounts receivable that are not worked systematically — with defined follow-up timelines, payer-specific escalation paths, and write-off authorization procedures — accumulate into losses that are difficult to recover.

GAKK reviews payment posting standards and accounts receivable management procedures, identifies gaps in current practice, and develops governance frameworks that produce accurate financial data and systematic follow-up on outstanding balances.

Billing Governance

The billing department in an FQHC needs operational infrastructure that most billing departments do not have: procedure documentation aligned to encounter rate rules; payer-specific submission standards written as executable procedures rather than held in staff knowledge; an internal review schedule that identifies process drift; and integration with documentation and intake workflows so that billing is not absorbing failures that originate upstream.

GAKK designs billing governance frameworks that cover all of these areas. The deliverable is not a set of recommendations — it is a documented, executable, staff-operable system.

Documentation & Intake Alignment

Billing performance is a downstream indicator. When denial rates are high, when first-pass resolution is low, when accounts receivable is aging, the cause almost always includes upstream failures in documentation or intake that billing cannot correct on its own. Billing that receives poorly documented encounters will produce denials regardless of billing quality. Billing that receives encounters with unverified eligibility will produce eligibility-related denials regardless of submission accuracy.

GAKK treats billing governance as one layer in an integrated operational substrate. Billing work is designed to connect with documentation governance and intake architecture — not as parallel projects, but as a coordinated operational system in which each layer reinforces the others.

Dental Billing

Dental billing in an FQHC operates under a different set of rules than general dental practice billing — and under a different set of rules than medical FQHC billing. The payer mix includes Medicaid dental, CHIP, sliding fee scale patients, and dual-coverage scenarios that require coordination of benefits handled correctly. CDT coding, ADA claim formatting, frequency limitations, missing teeth exclusions, and downcoding disputes are routine sources of lost revenue. General dental billers without FQHC experience consistently leave money on the table.

GAKK manages the full ADA claim lifecycle — submission, tracking, ERA/EOB posting, and payer follow-up — with FQHC-specific compliance built in. This includes medical-dental crossover billing for qualifying diagnoses, sliding fee scale application and documentation review in compliance with HRSA requirements, CDT coding accuracy review before submission, and denial management targeting the most common FQHC dental rejections. Coordination of benefits for patients with dual coverage, Medicaid, and CHIP is handled to maximize reimbursement without compliance risk.
