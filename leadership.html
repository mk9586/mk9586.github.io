Founders & Leadership

GAKK Consulting was built by practitioners who have worked inside the operational problems it now solves.

Founder & Clinical/Billing SME

Alice

Alice founded GAKK Consulting after years of direct operational work inside FQHCs and community health organizations — not as an outside consultant, but as the person responsible for billing operations, denial resolution, documentation compliance, and provider performance. Her background spans clinical documentation governance, revenue cycle management, denial pattern analysis, provider coding and documentation coaching, and FQHC operational governance in high-complexity environments.

She founded GAKK because she recognized a pattern that repeated across organizations: the failures destroying revenue and destabilizing operations in community health were structural, not personnel failures. Denials were not caused by careless billers. Documentation drift was not caused by indifferent providers. Intake failures were not caused by undertrained front desk staff. They were caused by systems that were never designed to hold under the operational conditions of an FQHC — payer complexity, eligibility volatility, sliding fee obligations, and documentation standards that changed faster than internal processes could adapt.

Alice's work at GAKK centers on clinical documentation governance, billing substrate design, denial pattern analysis, and direct provider documentation coaching. She works with clinical and billing teams directly, not above them.

Systems Architect

Mike

Mike joined GAKK as its systems architect, bringing a background in operational architecture, audit system design, intake and eligibility process engineering, and governance framework development. His work focuses on the structural layer: designing the substrates that make clinical, billing, and intake workflows deterministic and auditable.

Where Alice works from the operational and clinical reality of the problem, Mike works from the structural design of the solution. His role is to take the failure conditions Alice identifies and build the frameworks, audit logic, and workflow architectures that address them at the root.

Mike is not a clinician or billing specialist. He does not diagnose revenue cycle problems. He designs the systems that prevent them from recurring.

How We Work Together

Alice and Mike operate from different starting points, and that is intentional. Alice enters an engagement from the operational reality — what is actually failing, why it is failing, and what clinical and billing behaviors are contributing to it. Mike enters from the structural design — what architecture is needed to make corrected behaviors durable.

Neither discipline works without the other. A billing governance framework that is structurally sound but clinically unrealistic will not be followed. An operational correction that is accurate but unstructured will not hold. GAKK's engagements are built around the intersection of these two orientations.

The Problem Alice Set Out to Solve

Alice spent years watching the same operational failures repeat across different organizations. Denial rates that climbed despite remediation efforts. Documentation that improved temporarily after training and then drifted back within months. Intake processes that worked until they didn't, with no way to trace why. Revenue cycle teams blamed for outcomes they had no structural authority to prevent.

The common thread was not incompetence. It was the absence of governance infrastructure — documented, auditable, staff-executable frameworks that held even when personnel changed, payers changed, or clinical volumes increased. GAKK was founded to build that infrastructure, specifically for the organizations where it is hardest to build and where its absence causes the most damage.

Systems Architecture. Operational Governance.
