## 2. ABOUT (about.html)

**Page title:** About — GAKK Consulting

### About GAKK Consulting

GAKK Consulting is a systems architecture and operational governance firm. We work with FQHCs and community health organizations to build structured operational substrates — audit frameworks, documentation governance systems, intake architectures, and workflow stabilization procedures — that address the conditions producing revenue loss and compliance exposure.

### Our Doctrine

We approach every engagement governance-first. That means we do not recommend process changes without first understanding the structural conditions that produced the current state. We build deterministic systems — procedures and frameworks with defined inputs, defined decision logic, and traceable outputs — rather than guidance that depends on individual judgment to hold. Our work is substrate-aligned: every deliverable is designed to function within the operational reality of the organization, not against it.

### Why FQHCs

FQHCs operate under payer complexity that most health systems do not face: sliding fee schedules, encounter rate billing, UDS reporting requirements, managed care contract variation, and coverage populations with high eligibility volatility. These conditions create documentation drift, intake instability, and denial patterns that standard revenue cycle approaches are not designed to address. GAKK Consulting's work is built specifically for this environment.

### Billing & Revenue Cycle

Billing is not separate from operations — it is the endpoint of every documentation, intake, and eligibility decision made earlier in the encounter lifecycle. When billing fails at scale, the cause is almost never the billing department alone. GAKK Consulting treats revenue cycle performance as a downstream indicator of upstream structural conditions, and our work addresses both: the substrate problems that generate billing failures and the billing governance frameworks needed to manage them once the substrate is stable.

### How We Work

The core of our practice is sequencing and architecture. We identify the structural conditions driving operational problems, design the substrate needed to address them, and sequence implementation in a way that is executable within the organization's current capacity. We do not deliver reports that require external interpretation. We deliver frameworks that the organization can operate, audit, and maintain.

## 3. SERVICES (services.html)

**Page title:** Services — GAKK Consulting

GAKK Consulting works across five operational domains common to FQHCs and community health organizations.

### FQHC Audit Substrate

FQHCs routinely discover documentation deficiencies at the denial stage, after the clinical encounter has closed and the opportunity for correction has passed. Audit processes that run after the fact produce reports but not resolution.

- Designs pre-submission audit structures that flag documentation gaps at the point of encounter or shortly after
- Aligns audit logic to FQHC-specific billing requirements, including encounter rate rules and payer-specific documentation standards
- Establishes repeatable audit workflows that staff can operate without external consulting dependency

### Documentation & Coding Governance

Documentation quality in community health settings degrades over time when coding standards are applied inconsistently across providers, sites, or payer types. Inconsistency produces both compliance exposure and revenue leakage.

- Establishes documentation governance frameworks aligned to FQHC payer contracts, UDS reporting obligations, and clinical workflow constraints
- Identifies provider-level and site-level documentation variance and structures corrective guidance
- Builds internal review processes that maintain consistency without relying on continuous external oversight

### Intake & Eligibility Architecture

Intake and eligibility failures are often treated as front-desk errors when they are structural problems — gaps in process design that produce recurring downstream denials, coverage lapses, and patient account instability.

- Maps current intake and eligibility workflows against denial patterns to identify structural failure points
- Designs intake substrates that enforce eligibility verification, coverage sequencing, and sliding fee determination at the correct workflow stage
- Builds staff-executable procedures that reduce eligibility-related denials without adding administrative overhead

### Billing & Revenue Cycle Support

Revenue cycle failures in FQHCs are frequently diagnosed as billing department problems when they originate upstream — in documentation gaps, eligibility lapses, and workflow misalignment that reach the claim in an uncorrectable state.

- Reviews the claim lifecycle from encounter documentation through adjudication to identify failure points by payer, provider, and service type
- Structures billing workflows to align with FQHC encounter rate requirements, managed care contracts, and payer-specific submission rules
- Develops internal billing governance procedures that reduce rework, improve first-pass resolution, and create a traceable path from encounter to payment

### EHR Workflow Stabilization

EHR implementations frequently drift from their intended configuration as clinical staff adapt workflows informally over time. This drift produces documentation inconsistency, billing misalignment, and reporting gaps that are difficult to trace without structural analysis.

- Audits current EHR workflow configuration against intended clinical and billing design
- Identifies workflow deviations that contribute to documentation gaps, coding errors, or billing failures
- Develops corrective configuration and workflow guidance aligned to FQHC operational requirements

## 6. BILLING & REVENUE CYCLE (billing-revenue-cycle.html)

**Page title:** Billing & Revenue Cycle — GAKK Consulting

Revenue cycle failure in an FQHC is rarely a billing department problem in isolation. It is an operational alignment problem — documentation, intake, eligibility, and billing workflows that were never designed to work together under FQHC-specific conditions. GAKK addresses the full cycle.

### Claim Submission

FQHC claim submission operates under rules that do not apply in standard fee-for-service environments: encounter rate billing, prospective payment settlement, managed care encounter submission requirements, and payer-specific variations that differ by contract. Claims that are technically correct by general billing standards can still be denied or underpaid in an FQHC if they are not aligned to the specific requirements of the applicable payer and contract.

GAKK reviews claim submission workflows against the organization's actual payer mix and contract terms, identifies submission logic gaps, and develops submission standards that account for FQHC-specific requirements by payer type. This is not a billing department replacement — it is the governance layer that ensures the billing department is working from accurate, current, and documented submission standards.

### Denial Management

Denial management in an FQHC is a diagnostic function before it is a resolution function. The first step is not appeals — it is classification. Denials recorded only as denied produce no useful information. Denials classified by root cause, payer, provider, service type, and denial reason code produce the data needed to determine whether the failure originated in documentation, intake, eligibility, coding, or submission.

GAKK builds denial management systems that classify denials at the root cause level, route correction to the appropriate part of the organization, and track denial patterns over time to identify systemic failures. Appeals management is one component of this system — it is not the whole system.

### Payment Posting & Accounts Receivable

Payment posting errors and accounts receivable mismanagement compound quietly. Incorrect payment posting produces misleading performance data, distorts accounts receivable aging, and allows underpayment to go undetected. Accounts receivable that are not worked systematically — with defined follow-up timelines, payer-specific escalation paths, and write-off authorization procedures — accumulate into losses that are difficult to recover.

GAKK reviews payment posting standards and accounts receivable management procedures, identifies gaps in current practice, and develops governance frameworks that produce accurate financial data and systematic follow-up on outstanding balances.

### Billing Governance

The billing department in an FQHC needs operational infrastructure that most billing departments do not have: procedure documentation aligned to encounter rate rules; payer-specific submission standards written as executable procedures rather than held in staff knowledge; an internal review schedule that identifies process drift; and integration with documentation and intake workflows so that billing is not absorbing failures that originate upstream.

GAKK designs billing governance frameworks that cover all of these areas. The deliverable is not a set of recommendations — it is a documented, executable, staff-operable system.

### Documentation & Intake Alignment

Billing performance is a downstream indicator. When denial rates are high, when first-pass resolution is low, when accounts receivable is aging, the cause almost always includes upstream failures in documentation or intake that billing cannot correct on its own. Billing that receives poorly documented encounters will produce denials regardless of billing quality. Billing that receives encounters with unverified eligibility will produce eligibility-related denials regardless of submission accuracy.

GAKK treats billing governance as one layer in an integrated operational substrate. Billing work is designed to connect with documentation governance and intake architecture — not as parallel projects, but as a coordinated operational system in which each layer reinforces the others.
